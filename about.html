Visual artist, performer and researcher.
Born in Lisbon, Portugal, lives in London, UK.

Cristina Assunção is a multi-disciplinary artist. Her work brings attention to interrelations inner affects and its potentialities to act. In site-specific assemblages conducted though careful listening and observation she sets up and nurtures places for questioning and for dialogue.

Alongside such practice she has been evolving on dance without master, hinged as an ideal laboratory for interrelations: between objects and ideas that express contemporary art in the public realm and (re)call a circularity of artist, work, citizen and place.".

In 2017 she curated Hoje como ontem diferente de amanhã, an exhibition and series of activities within the Art+Feminism Edit-a-thon project. In the same year she co-curated the project Incerta desambiguação at Zaratan, Lisbon. In 2016 she took part on the production of Christo and Jeanne-Claude's The Floating Piers in Lago d'Iseo and in 2019 she assisted the production of Georgina Starr's Quarantaine.
