 	<p>Visual artist, performer and researcher.<br>Born in Lisbon, Portugal, lives in London, UK.</p>
	<p style="text-align:justify;"><!-- about the artistic practice in general-->Cristina Assun&ccedil;&atilde;o is a multi-disciplinary artist. Her work brings attention to interrelations inner affects and its potentialities to act. In site-specific assemblages conducted though careful listening and observation she sets up and nurtures places for questioning and for dialogue.<br>

	<p style="text-align:justify;"><!-- about performing and dance -->Alongside such practice she has been evolving on dance without master, hinged as an ideal laboratory for interrelations: between objects and ideas that express contemporary art in the public realm and (re)call a circularity of artist, work, citizen and place.&quot;.</p>

	<p style="text-align:justify;"><!-- about acting as a curator, producer and educator: what is my vision? -->In 2017 she curated <i>Hoje como ontem diferente de amanh&atilde;</i>, an exhibition and series of activities within the Art+Feminism Edit-a-thon project. In the same year she co-curated the project <i>Incerta desambigua&ccedil;&atilde;o</i> at Zaratan, Lisbon.

In 2016 she took part on the production of Christo and Jeanne-Claude&#39;s <i>The Floating Piers</i> in Lago d&#39;Iseo and in 2019 she assisted the production of Georgina Starr&#39;s <i>Quarantaine</i>.
</p>
